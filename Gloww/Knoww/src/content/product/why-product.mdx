here's the thing, i've been writing code for years. and i'm good at it (i think). but somewhere along the way i realized something:

**writing code and building products are two completely different skills.**

## the moment it clicked

i was working on this analytics feature at GEP. technically complex, DAX to SQL migration, 180+ customers, the whole thing. and i nailed the engineering.

but then someone asked: "why are we building this?"

and i realized... i had a STRONG opinion about that. not just "because the ticket says so" but actual thoughts about:
- why the current UX was confusing
- what users actually needed vs what they were asking for
- how the migration could unlock features we couldn't build before

that's when i knew. the code was the vehicle, but the product thinking was what actually excited me.

## what i mean by "product thinking"

it's not about being a PM. it's about asking:
- **who** is this for?
- **why** does it matter to them?
- **what** makes this better than what exists?
- **how** will we know it worked?

you can ask these questions as an engineer, a designer, or a PM. the role doesn't matter. the thinking does.
